02 / The Model

The Stakeholder Framework

A strategic framework that equips leaders with a practical, proven, and intentional approach to developing team members into mission-minded stakeholders through people-centered leadership and faithful stewardship of gifts.

This model combines role-specific career paths with systematic performance growth that aligns with objective skill development, mastery, and team-based collaboration in service of mission.

Recognition Structure: The fundamentals are the same for every team member ‚Äî growth in skill, experience, and collaboration drives career trajectory. This framework gives every person in the organization a clear path forward and includes certificates of recognition when goals are met.

A Coaching Organization: When this framework is implemented, leaders and supervisors become performance coaches, creating a culture of continuous development, care, accountability, and support at every level.

08 / Review System

Monthly Check-ins, Not Annual Surprises

Each month, the employee brings a self-evaluation to the supervisor ‚Äî rating their progress toward total completion of each goal on a 1‚Äì10 scale.

The supervisor affirms or gently redirects, then asks: "How can I support you and your progress this month?"

Key Insight: It's far more comfortable for a supervisor to gently correct a self-assessment monthly than to prepare once a year to address shortcomings. Most people naturally underestimate their progress, so supervisors are often able to encourage, affirm, and build them up.

09 / Performance & Recognition

Performance Drives Visibility

Whether someone is hourly or salaried, their contribution is driven by the same fundamentals: skill set, experience, and wisdom faithfully applied. High performance ensures that an individual's contributions are seen, valued, and celebrated across the organization.

90% ‚Üí 90%

The 90% ‚Üí 90% Principle: The percentage of goal achievement is directly tied to the level of formal recognition and professional endorsement. Achieve 90% of your goals, and you unlock 90% of the Recognition Tier for that period (e.g., public praise, internal awards, or leadership spotlights).

Growth in Standing: Staying on track to hit goals ensures that growing skill and collaboration translate into increased internal influence, priority for plum assignments, and first-look status for future promotions or leadership tracks.

Market-Based Benchmarking
Instead of benchmarking strictly against salary data, the organization uses objective data to benchmark Role Excellence. We define what Top Tier performance looks like in the industry, ensuring our people are becoming the most sought-after professionals in their field.
Getting Back on Track
If someone falls short of 100%, supervisors provide immediate, clear coaching. Because this system is built on recognition, the reward for getting back on track is the restoration of status and trust, ensuring the individual remains a Gold Standard contributor to the mission.
The Bottom Line
This system gives people ownership of their professional reputation and career trajectory. It ensures that the mission is fueled by people who are motivated by mastery and appreciation, creating a culture of excellence that precedes financial rewards.
